Essential Arbitrage Glossary

Arbitrage (Surebet):
The practice of taking advantage of a price difference between two or more markets to lock in a guaranteed profit regardless of the outcome.
Moneyline Odds:
Commonly used in the US, these odds are based on a $100 stake. Positive numbers (+) indicate how much profit you make on a $100 bet, while negative numbers (-) show how much you must bet to win $100.
Implied Probability:
The conversion of betting odds into a percentage. To find an arbitrage opportunity, the sum of implied probabilities must be less than 100%.
Stake Distribution:
The mathematical calculation of how much money to place on each outcome to ensure the same profit margin on both sides.
Market Spread:
The difference between the highest buy price and the lowest sell price. Narrower spreads in high-liquidity markets require faster calculation tools.
Value Betting:
Placing a bet when the probability of an event occurring is higher than what the bookmaker's odds suggest.
