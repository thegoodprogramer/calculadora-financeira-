What is the Kelly Criterion?

The Kelly Criterion is a mathematical formula used to determine the optimal size of a series of bets. In the world of sports betting and finance, it helps you maximize long-term wealth while minimizing the risk of ruin (going broke).

How to use this tool:

  1. Decimal Odds: Enter the odds offered by the bookmaker (e.g., 2.00 for even money).
  2. Probability: Enter your estimated chance of winning (from 0 to 100%). Tip: If you think it's a 50/50 coin toss, enter 50.
  3. Bankroll: Your total available funds for betting.

Why use it?

If the result is negative, it means you don't have an "edge" and you should not bet. If it's positive, the formula tells you exactly how much of your bankroll to risk to grow it as fast as possible mathematically.
